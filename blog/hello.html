They don't tell you that preparing for an IPO feels less like a celebration and more like constructing your own cage. Every number scrutinized. Every word lawyered. Every decision now carrying the weight of fiduciary duty to people you'll never meet.

I built this company in a garage with two laptops and a credit card limit I probably shouldn't have maxed. We moved fast because there was nothing to break except our own time and sanity. The board was three people around a kitchen table. Decisions took minutes, not months.
